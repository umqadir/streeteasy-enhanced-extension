Methodology

Data Sources

  • Crime Data: NYPD Complaint Data via NYC Open Data
  • Geography: NYC Neighborhood Tabulation Areas (NTAs)
  • Population: ACS 5-year estimates

What We Measure

Count: Total number of reported incidents in the time period

Rate: Incidents per 100,000 residents (allows comparison between neighborhoods of different sizes)

Percentile: What percentage of NYC neighborhoods have a lower rate (higher percentile = safer relative to other areas)

Rank: Position among all NYC neighborhoods (1 = lowest crime rate)

Crime Categories

  • Murder: Murder & non-negligent manslaughter
  • Felony Assault: Serious assaults causing injury or involving weapons

Important Limitations

⚠️ Complaint data ≠ actual victimization risk

  • Reflects reported complaints, not all incidents
  • Some crime types (e.g., sexual assaults) may not be geocoded in public data
  • Reporting rates vary by neighborhood
  • NTA boundaries don't match "real" neighborhood boundaries
  • Population estimates may be outdated for rapidly changing areas
  • Business districts may show high rates but low residential risk

Why Both Rates and Counts?

We show both rates (per 100k population) and absolute counts/ranks because:

  • Rates adjust for population size, enabling fair comparison
  • Counts show actual incident volume, important for low-population areas like business districts that may have high rates but moderate absolute crime levels
  • Together they give a more complete picture than either metric alone

About NTAs

Neighborhood Tabulation Areas (NTAs) are statistical geographies created by NYC Planning for Census data reporting. They approximate neighborhoods but are designed for statistical consistency, not to match subjective neighborhood boundaries.
